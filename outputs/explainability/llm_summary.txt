## Reliability & Risks
- **Imbalance Risk**: The model predicts only the "NO" class, indicating an imbalance issue that could affect its reliability.
- **Uncertainty**: The low accuracy and high confidence in a single class suggest potential overfitting. This uncertainty needs addressing for better predictions.
- **Potential Leakage Checks**: Evaluate features for possible data leakage to ensure they don’t include future information that could bias predictions.

## Action Plan
1. **Address Class Imbalance**: Implement techniques like oversampling, undersampling, or synthetic data generation to balance prediction classes.
2. **Enhance Feature Selection**: Review features to eliminate any that may lead to data leakage or are not contributing positively to predictions.
3. **Model Retraining**: Experiment with different algorithms or model parameters to improve accuracy and evaluation metrics.
4. **Increase Training Data**: Collect more diverse patient data to enhance model learning and generalization.
5. **Regular Monitoring**: Establish ongoing monitoring of model performance to detect shifts in data distribution and recalibrate as necessary.
